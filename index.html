Trust & custody design

In modern finance, the biggest risk is not always volatility — it’s control concentration. Reytech Reserve is designed around a simple trust rule: user funds should not depend on one person.

Custodial-first approach

The model assumes that, especially in early stages, user funds are held with licensed third-party custodial partners (where applicable). This reduces single-point control risk and increases accountability.

  • ✓Clear separation between system operations and fund custody
  • ✓Transparency about where funds are held (as partnerships mature)
  • ✓Operational rules designed to be auditable

Transparency by default

We prefer clarity over complexity. Trust is built with: published rules, visible fees, and a system that explains itself.

  • ✓Plain language and publicly visible terms
  • ✓No “secret” investor rules or hidden conditions
  • ✓Updates shared openly (see X and Telegram)
